Part 1: Introduction & Foundational Setup

1.1 Project Vision & Goals (Parallax & Kubrik)

  • Overall Vision: Build Kubrik, a sophisticated AI film director, able to autonomously create distinctive horror films, and launch Parallax as the user-facing MVP to test and fund the vision.
  • Parallax Goals: Launch a B2C web app for users to swap their faces into AI-generated horror shorts, providing feedback and validating technical/market fit.
  • Kubrik Goals: Long-term phased development toward a fully autonomous AI director capable of interpreting prompts, scriptwriting, visual design, editing, and sound.
  • Guiding Principles: User-centric MVP, modular AI system, rapid iteration, scalable design, responsible AI ethics, and creative human oversight.

1.2 Project Management Tool Setup (ClickUp)

  • Why ClickUp? Customizable, scalable, future-proof for team growth, offers a generous free tier, and suitable for non-technical founders.
  • Setup Steps: Create "Parallax MVP" and "Kubrik R&D" spaces. Use lists, folders, and docs for all modules, experiments, and documentation. Adopt templates and link tasks for project clarity.

1.3 Initial Resource & Tool Assessment

  • Core Skills: Film/video editing, creative direction, project management, basic CS understanding, ability to guide AI tools and assistants.
  • Key Tools: ClickUp, Runway Gen-2, Pika Labs, CapCut, Magic Hour, Carrd/Typedream, Google Drive, Airtable/Notion, ElevenLabs, GA4, etc.
  • Budget: Ultra-lean. <$100/month first year (max free tiers, minimal subscriptions).
Part 2: Integrated Development Roadmap - Phased Execution

Chronological plan for Parallax MVP launch and Kubrik R&D:

  1. Phase 1: Parallax MVP (face-swapping horror shorts) - validate, gather feedback, build credibility.
  2. Phase 2: Iterate on Parallax, begin Kubrik core module experiments (script generation, visual prompts, asset mgmt).
  3. Phase 3: Deeper Kubrik prototyping, integrate with Parallax, focus on iterative video generation and curation.
  4. Phase 4: Advanced Kubrik assembly/editing (HAL), tone enforcement, first end-to-end Kubrik short using the full workflow.
  5. Phase 5: Strategic review and alpha launch of Kubrik modules for wider testing/funding push.
Part 3: Kubrik Core Modules - Detailed Guides
  • Iris: Prompt engineering & script generation (LLMs, style guide integration)
  • Monolith: Visual storytelling, translating scripts to AI video prompts
  • Iterative Video Generation & Curation: Batch generation, curation/rating, iterative improvement
  • Intelligent Asset Management: Airtable/Notion/ClickUp, detailed metadata & tagging
  • HAL: Automated/manual assembly, editing rules, FFmpeg/EDL experiments
  • Kubrik Personality: Tone enforcement framework, comprehensive style guide, feedback loops
Part 4: Strategic Considerations & Future Outlook
  • Content Ownership & Legal: Clear copyright for templates, terms of service for user uploads, careful with AI tool TOS and data use.
  • Monetization: Start free, move to freemium/subscription. Explore B2B SaaS/licensing and asset marketplaces for Kubrik.
  • Scalability: Modular, swappable architecture; continuous research & integration of new tools; design for future autonomy.
  • Team & Funding: Identify future roles (AI/ML engineers, devs, UX, prompt engineers, BD/sales). Use Parallax/Kubrik v0.x as investor pitch assets.
